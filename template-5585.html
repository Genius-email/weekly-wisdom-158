When we were little, we knew who we were. 

 

We knew what we liked and what gave us joy, and we unapologetically gave these things to ourselves without hesitation. We knew how to enjoy life. 

 

Getting lost in a book was simple and marvelous. Just like you, I could finish an entire book in one day. 

 

When we got older, things became more complicated. We became aware that the world expected so much of us. 

 

We started doing things not simply for joy or love, but because we thought we SHOULD (to please others, to conform to society, to make our parents happy and take care of our loved ones). 

 

Suddenly, joy wasn’t part of the equation anymore. Work and money and responsibility took up all our time. And when we weren’t out in the world trying to get everything on our to-do lists done, we were too tired to do the things we once loved. 

 

But when you lose touch with what makes you happy, you partially lose yourself. 

 

The solution is found in remembering who you are. 

 

You need to relearn what it feels like to honor yourself after years and years of living for other people. 

 

It’s time to get back in touch with the things you once loved so deeply. Create a list of daily rituals that feed your soul. Focus less on your outer life and more on your inner life. Remember what once made you feel loved and alive, and try to get back to that feeling. 

 

If the world expected NOTHING of you… what would you do every day? Who would you choose to be? How would you give yourself a sense of love, peace, and validation?

 

I want you to…

 

✨ Clear your Sunday schedule and get away from the noise. 

✨ Turn off your phone. 

✨ Open that beautiful new book.

✨ Allow yourself to get lost in its pages. Be fully present with it.  

✨ Remind yourself that this is something you’re doing just for YOU. 

 

Reading a book may sound simple and small. It’s not. It’s everything. It’s you relearning what it means to do things just for the joy of it. It’s the understanding that you can alway find your way back to yourself, no matter how far you wander. 

 

May you spread well-being everywhere you go,
